As … As
We use as … as to say two things are equal or
the same. We use as + adjective + as with nouns.
• London is as big as Paris.
• I’m as old as Jim.
We use (not) as … as to say two things are not
the same.
• I’m not as old as Sarah. Sarah’s 13. I’m 12.
When a pronoun follows as … as, we use the
object form.
• Jim is as old as me.
• I’m as fast as them.
We can also use as … as to compare gerunds
(-ing).
• Running is as good as jogging.
• Dancing isn’t as popular as swimming.
